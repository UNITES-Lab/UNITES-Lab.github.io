About PI

Professor Tianlong Chen [Google Scholar] is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science at The University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, where he directs the UNITES (University of North Carolina, AI Trustworthiness, Efficiency, and for Science) Lab. He received his Ph.D. degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering from University of Texas at Austin in 2023, and completed his postdoctoral research at MIT CSAIL and Harvard Medical School in 2023-2024.

Dr. Chen's research focuses on building accurate, trustworthy, and efficient machine learning systems. His work spans fundamental algorithms for sparse neural networks, scalable optimization methods, and responsible AI systems. He has made significant contributions to the lottery ticket hypothesis, learning to optimize frameworks, and efficient deep learning methods. His research has been applied to various domains including healthcare, scientific computing, and large language models.

Dr. Chen has broad research interests in machine learning (ML), optimization, and AI for science. Currently, his research centers on developing efficient and trustworthy AI systems, with particular emphasis on sparse neural networks, efficient large language models, and AI for scientific applications. His work aims to make AI systems more accessible, reliable, and computationally efficient while maintaining high performance. Dr. Chen's research has been supported by NSF, NIH, Amazon Research Awards, Cisco Faculty Awards, UNC Accelerating AI Awards, and NAIRR Pilot Awards.

Dr. Chen has received numerous awards and honors, including the Amazon Research Award (Fall 2024), Cisco Faculty Award, AAAI'25 New Faculty Highlights, NAIRR Pilot Award, UNC Accelerating AI Awards, CPAL Rising Star Award, KAUST Rising Stars Award, AdvML Rising Star Award, Low-Resource Methods for NLP SAC Award at NAACL 2025, Marco Ramoni Distinguished Paper Award at AMIA-IS 2025, Best Paper Award at LoG 2022, Best Paper Award at GenAI4Health at AAAI 2025, and Best Demo Paper Award at GenAI4Health at NeurIPS 2024. He has also received prestigious fellowships including the IBM Ph.D. Fellowship, Adobe Ph.D. Fellowship, and Graduate Dean's Prestigious Fellowship. Dr. Chen is actively recruiting talented students to join the UNITES Lab.

About Our Research

At the UNITES Lab, we pursue cutting-edge research in machine learning and artificial intelligence, with a focus on building systems that are accurate, trustworthy, and efficient. Our research spans theoretical foundations to practical applications, with particular emphasis on making AI systems more accessible and reliable for real-world deployment.

Our current work is organized around three key themes: efficient deep learning, trustworthy AI, and AI for science. Throughout these areas, we maintain a commitment to developing algorithms that are both theoretically sound and practically impactful. We collaborate closely with industry partners and other academic institutions to ensure our work addresses pressing technological and societal needs.

Theme 1: Efficient Deep Learning - Sparsity, Compression, and Scalable Training

We focus on advancing the efficiency, scalability and trust of LLMs through innovative approaches to training and inference. Our research explores memory-efficient LLM training techniques (APOLLO, GaLoRe, & LiGO), efficient generative inference methods (H2O & Flextron), understanding pre-trained model weights (lottery ticket) or training artifacts (oversmoothening & LLM-PBE): many accompanied with system or hardware co-design.

    • Theme 2: Trustworthy AI - Robustness, Fairness, and Interpretability

    Our research in trustworthy AI focuses on making machine learning systems more robust, fair, and interpretable. We develop methods for adversarial robustness, certified defenses, and fairness-aware learning. Our work includes techniques for understanding model decisions, detecting and mitigating biases, and ensuring reliable deployment in safety-critical applications.

    • Theme 3: AI for Science - Healthcare, Biology, and Scientific Computing

    Our AI for Science research applies machine learning techniques to accelerate scientific discovery across multiple domains. We develop specialized AI methods for healthcare applications, biological sequence analysis, protein engineering, and quantum computing. Our work aims to bridge the gap between AI capabilities and real-world scientific challenges, creating tools that can augment human expertise in scientific research.
